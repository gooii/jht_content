UK Medical Heritage Library

Search and view the collection of over 66,000 digitised European medical publications from the nineteenth century

Royal College of Physicians

A professional membership body for physicians, its library was founded in 1518. As well as publications on health and disease, the collection covers topics as diverse as zoology, architecture, travel, history, poetry and religion, donated by those physicians who had interests beyond medicine.

Approximately 17,000 books and pamphlets were within the date range of this project. Highlights include the history of the College, and items written by its fellows and members including examination papers and lecture transcripts. Other strengths include digitised books by Alfred Swaine Taylor, the 'father of British toxicology', debates surrounding the risks of vaccination, and occupational health in diverse industries such as mining and pottery. Immunology and microbiology is represented through the works of the Nobel Prize winners Élie Metchnikoff and Paul Ehrlich. The collection also highlights the social history of the period: not least the practice of visiting health resorts documented through many travel guides for retreats and resorts like Bath or the Riviera. The RCP digitised collection includes some of the earliest works by women studying to become physicians such as Ann Elizabeth Clark, Edith Pechey-Phipson, and Sophia Jex-Blake. Another strength of the collection is the range of languages. While the majority of the items are in English, there are also books and pamphlets available in French, German, Latin, Spanish, Italian, Arabic, Swedish, Norwegian, Dutch and Tamil.

Access to the originals:
All of the original items have been kept by the RCP and are available to request through the library catalogue. Anyone wishing to access pre-1850 materials can do so by making a research appointment with the library. These can then be read in the library reading room. Post-1850 items can be viewed without a research appointment, though some need advance booking. Many 20th century items are available on the shelves of the library reading room. The library is open for the public to use for reference purposes for free, Monday to Friday, 9 to 5, excluding bank holidays.
