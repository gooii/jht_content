University of Glasgow

The University of Glasgow is the fourth oldest University in the United Kingdom, founded in 1451. The study of medicine was first mentioned in 1637 but the modern medical school came into being when William Cullen was appointed Professor of Medicine in 1751. The University also has a School of Veterinary Medicine, founded in 1862, and the Dental School, founded in 1878, is the second largest in the UK.

The Library’s general medical collections are particularly strong in anatomy, surgery, clinical medicine, public health, sanitation, and homeopathic medicine. The University was heavily involved with the development of a health care system in Glasgow, and in particular, with the treatment of mental illness and infectious diseases. The Library’s medical collections reflect this history. There is also a diverse and extensive collection of historical material ranging from vaccination, medical botany, and the treatment of consumption and cholera, to areas such as psychology, health spas, cookery, and alchemy. Within the University Library’s Special Collections there are outstanding resources for research on the history of medicine generally, and in the areas of anatomy, obstetrics and gynaecology in particular, largely owing to the superb library of William Hunter (1718-1783), a leading eighteenth century anatomist.

The University of Glasgow identified around 7,300 titles as part of the UK Medical Heritage Library Project, of which around 5,000 were sent for digitisation. Exclusions included volumes that were outsize or where their condition made them unsuitable for scanning or for transportation. There were a small number of titles not sent which fell within the scope of the project but which are part of the Library’s Hunterian Collection and were judged too valuable to be released. All of the original print materials can be consulted in the Library.

Access to the originals:
Original materials are available on request and can be consulted in the Library by members of the public. Details of access conditions and opening hours can be found on the main Library website. Details on the Hunterian Library can be found on the Special Collections website and specific pages relating to the William Hunter collection housed at the University of Glasgow. Titles can be searched via the Library catalogue. Quick Search is no longer used. Searching is from Library home page.
