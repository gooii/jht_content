Royal College of Physicians of Edinburgh

The Royal College of Physicians of Edinburgh was founded by Royal Charter in 1681. Its library and Archives now holds over 60,000 books, almost 1500 journals and over 300 externally deposited manuscript collections.

During the nineteenth century, the Royal College of Physicians of Edinburgh was one of the leading medical institutions in Great Britain. Like other medical corporations it had to adjust to the changes the century brought like the 1858 Medical Act, the enhanced value placed on basic scientific training of medical students, the development of modern examination structures and increasing specialisation. To support its role, the College collected widely and invested in a new library to house its growing book collection. It became the leading medical library in Scotland and regularly bought monographs to meet the contemporary medical information needs of its fellows.

Scientific medicine, public health, sociology and other disciplines all feature and as might be expected, the collection contains works by many of the leading figures of the time including Chadwick, Christison, Hahneman, Hughes-Bennett, Koch, Lister, Pasteur, Simpson, Syme, Tuke and Virchow. The College has carefully looked after its 19th century collection which was catalogued to modern standards in the 21st century thanks to Wellcome Trust Research Resources funding.

Access to the originals:
Researchers are very welcome to see originals from the UK Medical Heritage Library in the College's reading room. For opening hours and other access conditions, see the website. Library, journal and archive catalogues can be accessed online.
