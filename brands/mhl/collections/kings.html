King's College London

King's College London was founded in 1829 and opened in 1831: medicine was taught from the outset. A decade later, King's opened a new hospital, King's College Hospital, in nearby Portugal Street. This relocated to its current premises in Denmark Hill in south London, in 1913. The already merged medical schools of Guy's and St Thomas' Hospitals became part of King's in 1998. Particular strengths include the study of nursing, via the Nightingale School of Nursing, dental education, and psychiatry: the world-renowned Institute of Psychiatry merged with King's in 1997.

King's contribution to the UK Medical Heritage Library comprises 2,000 medical books, pamphlets and periodicals out of a total of 17,600 medical publications held in its Foyle Special Collections Library and reflects this rich tradition of medical teaching and research. Highlights include items from the historical book collection of the Institute of Psychiatry (founded 1923) and the historical library collection of the Foreign and Commonwealth Office (founded 1782, transferred to King's 2007).

The historical book collections of the medical schools of St Thomas' Hospital (founded 1551), Guy's Hospital (founded 1726) and King's College Hospital (founded 1840) are well represented, as is the library of the Guy's Hospital Physical Society (active 1771-1852).

King's collections are strong in many areas, and subjects covered include psychiatry and the care of the mentally ill; neurology; public health and sanitation, with a particular emphasis on public health administration in the former British Empire; tropical medicine; anatomy; surgery; general medicine and pathology; obstetrics and gynaecology; and popular medicine.

Access to the originals:
Original materials can be consulted in the Foyle Special Collections Library, Chancery Lane, London.
