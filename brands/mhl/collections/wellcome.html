Wellcome Library

The Wellcome Library - part of Wellcome Collection - is one of the world's major resources for the study of histories and cultures of medicine. It is developing a world-class online collection for the history of medicine by digitising a substantial proportion of its holdings and making the content freely available on the web: collections already digitised include the history of genetics, mental healthcare and London Medical Officer of Health reports.

The Library interprets 'medicine' quite broadly, to include related sciences, consumer health, sport and fitness, food and nutrition, as well as medical practices, such as mesmerism, phrenology and hydrotherapy, that have since fallen out of favour, but which were important in their time. The Wellcome Library has so far contributed over 12,000 monographs to the UK Medical Heritage Library, and will continue to add titles by the thousands over the next few years.

Access to the originals:

The original works are housed in the Library, based in the Wellcome Collection building in in central London. More information on the Library's location and opening hours is available from the website. The Library catalogue provides direct access to the digitised content from the UK Medical Heritage Library.
