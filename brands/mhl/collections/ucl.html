UCL Library Services

University College London was founded in 1826 in Bloomsbury and its General Library opened in 1849. The main Library currently holds collections in the Arts & Humanities, with a large Science Library close by. A further 14 specialist libraries support UCL's teaching and research across its campuses, including several that provide services to both UCL and the National Health Service. The Special Collections and Digital Collections are a rich source of manuscripts, archives and rare books.

UCL has supplied more than 2,500 texts from the Institute of Neurology, the Joint Library of Ophthalmology, Moorfields Eye Hospital & UCL Institute of Ophthalmology, the Institute of Orthopaedics, the School of Pharmacy and the Institute of Child Health libraries.

The major contribution is from publications on ophthalmology, the eye and its diseases, and their treatment including surgery. Other strengths include texts on diseases of the nervous system, mental illness and melancholy; embryology, developmental conditions and congenital illnesses; and books relating to Institutions including Miller Hospital and Royal Kent Dispensary, special hospitals and London hospitals. Other publications relate to the senses including colour blindness and perception, memory and learning and language, and anatomy including atlases of anatomy. Publications in German are well represented and comprise 30% of the total.
