Information for Librarians

This page is designed to provide key information to help librarians at subscribing institutions set up and manage access to Historical Texts. If you have a question that is not answered below please contact our helpdesk at historicaltexts@jisc.ac.uk and we will be happy to assist you.

Subscriptions

Historical Texts is available via subscription to UK Higher Education and Further Education institutions and Research Councils who are members of Jisc Collections. Eligible institutions can request a free 30 day trial of the resource. For any subscription enquiries please contact the Jisc Collections helpdesk at: help.digitalresources@jisc.ac.uk.

Authentication

We provide access via the UK Access Management Federation (Shibboleth/OpenAthens) as well as IP authentication. You can have one or both of these methods enabled. If you want to check the authentication options enabled for your institition for Historical Texts, or make any changes to them, please contact our helpdesk at historicaltexts@jisc.ac.uk

WAYFless URLs

We provide WAYFless URLs for Historical Texts.

You can use the WAYFless URL generator (WUGEN) tool to generate one for your institition.

  • Go to the WAYFless URL generator (WUGEN) tool
  • Select your institution as the "Identity Provider"
  • Select "Historical Texts" as the Service Provider
  • Accept the default target URL of "https://historicaltexts.jisc.ac.uk/"

A WAYFless URL will be displayed for your institution for you to copy or email to yourself.

Proxy servers

If you access Historical Texts using IP authentication, and you have a proxy server at your institution which you wish to be able to use for accessing the resource, please let our helpdesk know your proxy serversâ€™ IP address, if it is not included in the range you have provided for your institution.

You may also need to add the following entries to your proxy server forward table:

  • http://historicaltexts.jisc.ac.uk
  • https://historicaltexts.jisc.ac.uk
  • https://data.historicaltexts.jisc.ac.uk

If you use EZProxy then you can use the EZProxy stanza for Historical Texts which is available on the OCLC support pages.

Athens resource list

Historical Texts is available via UK Access Management Federation authentication (Shibboleth and OpenAthens) and it is not an Athens protected resource. To add it to your Athens resource list it will need to be added as an "additional resource" by your institutions Athens Administrator.

If you would like instructions on how to do this please contact the OpenAthens service desk.

MARC records

MARC records are available for the EEBO, ECCO I, ECCO II and BL collections. There are currently no MARC records for the Thomason Tracts and Thomason Tracts Broadsides parts of the service.
MARC records can be made available to subscribing institutions for ingest into library discovery services upon request. Please contact the Historical Texts helpdesk to request access.

Usage statistics

A usage statistics portal is available for Historical Texts.

The portal provides full text access statistics from the launch of the new platform on 23 June 2014.

The portal is available to librarians at subscribing institutions and more than one librarian at an institution can have a user account for it. Please contact our helpdesk at historicaltexts@jisc.ac.uk to request an account.

Support

We provide a Support section containing an introductory screencast, FAQs, a help page including a comparison of the features available by collection. For any further assistance please contact our helpdesk at historicaltexts@jisc.ac.uk.

Promotion

A promotional video is available on the service YouTube channel.

Maintenance

We schedule our routine maintenance work on Tuesday mornings between 07:00 and 09:00, where possible.

We provide 10 working days notice of any planned maintenance by emailing the Jisc eCollections JiscMail list to inform librarians at subscribing institutions, the Historical Texts JiscMail list , which is open to anyone, and putting a message in an orange alert box on our homepage as well as on Twitter.

Keeping up to date

All primary contacts at subscribing institutions are automatically added to the Jisc eCollections mailing list, where information for all three services in the Jisc eCollections service (Historical Texts, Journal Archives and MediaPlus) is posted. Additionally, there is an Historical Texts JiscMail list which is open to anyone who would like to keep up to date with the latest news, developments and events for the Historical Texts service. We also have Twitter and Facebook accounts.
