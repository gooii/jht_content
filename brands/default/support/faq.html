Frequently asked questions

Can I access Historical Texts?

Historical Texts is available via subscription to UK Higher Education and Further Education institutions and Research Councils who are full members of Jisc Collections. Eligible institutions can request a free 30 day trial of the resource. For any subscription enquiries please contact the Jisc Collections helpdesk at: help.digitalresources@jisc.ac.uk. Historical Texts is also available to access in the British Library reading rooms in London.

Troubleshooting tips - clearing your browser cache and cookies

As a first step, if you experience any problems using Historical Texts we would suggest you clear your browser cache and cookies then restart your browser. The Historical Texts application will save information in your browser, so if you've used the service previously, then clearing this information ensures you're seeing the latest version of the application. We would recommend clearing your browser cache and cookies after we release updates to the service for this reason.

Information on how to clear your cache and cookies in various browsers:

  • Internet Explorer 11
  • Firefox
  • Chrome
  • Safari

Do you provide WAYFless URLs?

Yes, we provide WAYFless URLs for Historical Texts.

You can use the WAYFless URL generator (WUGEN) tool to generate one for your institition.

  • Go to the WAYFless URL generator (WUGEN) tool
  • Select your institution as the "Identity Provider"
  • Select "Historical Texts" as the Service Provider
  • Accept the default target URL of "https://historicaltexts.jisc.ac.uk/"

A WAYFless URL will be displayed for your institution for you to copy or email to yourself.

Can subscribing institutions authenticate to Historical Texts via IP address?

Yes - providing your institution has a valid subscription to the Historical Texts service, we can set up access via IP address if requested by your library team. Please be aware that if you authenticate via IP and wish to use the Saved Searched or My Saved Texts features you will be prompted to sign in with your institutional credentials to allow the service to identify you as an individual user.

Are images and full text available for all collections?

Images are available for all three of our collections. Full text is available for ECCO, BL and for around a third of EEBO currently. Where full text is available "full text" will be clearly stated under the record on the results page. The data and features available varies by collection as each collection has been provided by a separate content provider. Details of these differences can be found on our features by collection page.

Can I search within a publication?

It is possible to search within a publication provided the full text for it is available. Having opened a publication in the Viewer, click the Search button to open the left-hand panel, and click the Pages tab. From here, you can enter search terms to be run on the current publication. Results for this search will be listed in the panel below the search box, and can be ordered by relevance, page ascending or descending using the drop-down arrow. Clicking a result will open it in the Viewer.

Can I download the texts?

Yes, you can download the titles in their entirety from all three collections in PDF format or individual images in JPEG format directly within the interface. Simply select a search result from the results list and open it in the viewer, then choose the download icon on the toolbar to download a PDF of the whole publication directly.

Please note that while PDFs for all three collections are available, only the PDFs for the BL collection are searchable and they have been provided by the British Library.

The Date Range Histogram results look weird?

The histogram represents the distribution of your search results. It's possible to drag-and-click on the graph to make finer selections. It's possible either with this selection, or by making very specific searches (ones that only yield a single result, for example) to get a solid bar shown on the histogram. As such, it's not a fault, it's just a reflection of the results and filtering of your search.

Why have I been signed out of Historical Texts unexpectedly?

Historical Texts will sign out inactive sessions after an hour, approximately. There are other factors that can also cause you to be signed out unexpectedly that are outside of our domain, including your institutional provider and the route they use to authenticate you, so this cannot be guaranteed as a fixed amount of time. Users are best advised to save any items or searches before leaving their computer for any extended period.
