Can I access Burney Content?

This content is part of the Historical Texts collection and is available via subscription to UK Higher Education and Further Education institutions and Research Councils who are full members of Jisc Collections. Eligible institutions can request a free 30 day trial of the resource. For any subscription enquiries please contact the Jisc Collections helpdesk at: help.digitalresources@jisc.ac.uk. Historical Texts is also available to access in the British Library reading rooms in London.

Troubleshooting tips

As a first step, if you experience any problems using Historical Texts we would suggest you clear your browser cache and cookies then restart your browser. The Historical Texts application will save information in your browser, so if you've used the service previously, then clearing this information ensures you're seeing the latest version of the application. We would recommend clearing your browser cache and cookies after we release updates to the service for this reason.

Information on how to clear your cache and cookies in various browsers:

  • Internet Explorer 11
  • Firefox
  • Chrome
  • Safari

The preferred route to access Historical Texts is as follows:

  • Browse to the following link: http://historicaltexts.jisc.ac.uk
  • Click the “Search Historical Texts” button
  • At the next page, search for your institution by name, select it from the list and click the “Continue” button
  • When you arrive at your institutional login page, enter your credentials

You should now be returned to the Historical Texts homepage, successfully authenticated.

If you're still experiencing difficulties, please contact the Historical Texts helpdesk at historicaltexts@jisc.ac.uk. Please include your institutional affiliation, which computer operating system you are using (e.g.Windows 7, Mac OSX etc), which web browsing software you are using (e.g.Chrome, Safari, Internet Explorer) and details of the route you are using to access the service (e.g. a link from your library service, a wayfless URL etc) and we'll be in touch to assist.

Can I search within a publication?

It is possible to search within a publication provided the full text for it is available. Having opened a publication in the Viewer, click the Search button to open the left-hand panel, and click the Pages tab. From here, you can enter search terms to be run on the current publication. Results for this search will be listed in the panel below the search box, and can be ordered by relevance, page ascending or descending using the drop-down arrow. Clicking a result will open it in the Viewer.

Can I browse the Burney collection?

The Historical Tests UI (on which this labs site is based) only supports browsing by author or printer/publisher however, this metadata is not present in the Burney collection. To provide users with a browse experience in the labs interface we have created the title list page in the about support section. This page simulates a browse by title functionality by applying filters to the collection content.

Can I download the texts?

Yes, you can download the titles in their entirety from the Burney collection in PDF format or individual images in JPEG format directly within the interface. Simply select a search result from the results list and open it in the viewer, then choose the download icon on the toolbar to download a PDF of the whole publication directly.

Why have I been signed out of Historical Texts unexpectedly?

Historical Texts will sign out inactive sessions after approximately an hour. There are other factors that can also cause you to be signed out unexpectedly that are outside of our domain, including your institutional provider and the route they use to authenticate you, so this cannot be guaranteed as a fixed amount of time. Users are best advised to save any items or searches before leaving their computer for any extended period.

How do I request permission to reproduce images from the Burney Collection?

The Burney Newspaper Collection data in this labs site has been provided by Cengage. The copyright for all images is owned by the publishers and so permission to reproduce them must be sought from them.

You do not need permission to use images in unpublished works such as theses unless they will be available on the open web or in an institutional repository or other database. To request permission to reproduce a work or to seek more information, contact the publishers directly:

Cengage have an online request form for this purpose. A link to the form can be found on the Cengage Rights and Permissions page

Why are my results greyed out?

Since the last open access release in June (which included efforts to make Historical Texts more mobile-friendly) some users have found that Historical Texts now displays in the mobile format when on a full screen. This layout includes a sidebar and the "greying out" of results which appear inaccessible. This can be resolved by clicking on the double chevrons near the thumbnails on/off button, minimising the menu and allowing access to the results.

Some users have found that by changing their screen resolution, the view reverts back to normal.
