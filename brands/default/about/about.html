Historical Texts: One platform, over 460,000 late 15th to long 19th century texts

This video has no narration and so has no closed captions. An audio description version of the video is avalable on our YouTube channel. You can also download the What is Historical Texts video transcript as a PDF/A compliant file.

Historical Texts is a full text digital archive enabling you to cross search, view and download over 460,000 texts published in the late C15th to the long C19th from four key collections:

  • Early English Books Online (EEBO) (1473-1700)
  • Eighteenth Century Collections Online (ECCO) (1701-1800)
  • 65,000 British Library 19th Century texts (BL) (1789-1914)
  • UK Medical Heritage Library (UKMHL) (1800s-1900s)

The resource encompasses a wealth of content ranging from the Romantic to the Victorian period and covers a wide range of subject areas including English literature, history, geography, science, social science, religion and medicine.

Materials include books but also pamphlets, sermons, prayer books, sheet music, broadsides, newsbooks and much more.

It's a Jisc service available via subscription to UK HE and FE institutions and Research Councils who are full members of Jisc Collections. Historical Texts is also available to access in the British Library reading rooms in London.

Key features

  • Cross-search EEBO, ECCO, UK Medical Heritage Library and 65,000 texts from the British Library C19th collection for the first time to explore themes
  • View and search the full text (where available) as well as the bibliographic information for texts
  • View images and full text side by side
  • Download whole publications (PDF) and individual images (JPEG)

Benefits to researchers

  • Time saving, reducing need to travel to holding libraries to view texts
  • Access to rare and, in some cases, previously inaccessible texts
  • Full text searching across the 65,000 texts from the BL C19th collection for the first time
  • View ECCO full text for the first time
  • Cross search EEBO, ECCO, UK Medical Heritage Library and 65,000 BL 19th century texts in one place to quickly discover related material
  • View full text and images side by side for first time
  • Discover new ways to explore the UK Medical Heritage Library via visualisations

Benefits to institutions

  • Historical Texts is included in the Jisc VAT cost sharing group, so for members of the group there is no VAT on the subscription fee
  • MARC records for EEBO, ECCO and the 65,000 texts from the BL collection are included with the subscription fee and available on request
  • A single, simple to use platform to support users on for EEBO, ECCO and the 65,000 texts from the BL collection

Open-access and subscriber content and features

Open-access content and features

Open-access content is available to all without subscription.

  • Full access to the UK Medical Heritage Library Collection (UKMHL) via the Labs area
  • Full Access to the Visualisation tools for the UKMHL collection

Subscriber content and features

Subscriber content is restricted to members of subscribing institutions.

  • Full access to the UK Medical Heritage Library (UKMHL) via the Labs area
  • Full Access to the Visualisation tools for the UKMHL collection
  • Full access to the four collections:
    • Early English Books Online (EEBO)
    • Eighteenth Century Collection Online (ECCO)
    • British Library 19th Century Collection (BL)
    • UK Medical Heritage Library (UKMHL)
  • Cross-search the EEBO, ECCO, UK Medical Heritage Library and BL collections
  • Personalisation features (Save searches and publications, set preferences on collections to search) for the primary collections.

Subscription options

The UK Medical Heritage Library is freely available. It can be accessed via the main platform or the separate dedicated UK Medical Heritage Library site which also provides visualisations to explore the content.

Subscription options vary for HE and FE institutions and a free 30 day trial is available to eligible institutions. The subscription fee is pro-rata, so if you subscribe part way through the subscription year you only pay for the remaining months for that year rather than the whole year.

For any enquiries about subscription options please contact the Jisc Collections helpdesk at: help.digitalresources@jisc.ac.uk.

A librarian at the institution can do this by contacting the Jisc Collections helpdesk at: help.digitalresources@jisc.ac.uk or logging in to their account on the Jisc Collections website.

Background

Find out more about why the Jisc Historic Books, Jisc Journal Archives and Jisc MediaHub services were set up originally and some of the challenges we encountered along the way in this Insights article by Caren Milloy (Head of Projects, Jisc Collections) : http://insights.uksg.org/articles/10.1629/2048-7754.25.1.74/

  • Jisc Historic Books was replaced by the Historical Texts platform in June 2014.
  • Jisc Journal Archives was replaced by the Journal Archives platform (https://journalarchives.jisc.ac.uk/) in January 2015.
  • Jisc MediaHub was decommissioned in September 2016 (https://www.jisc.ac.uk/mediahub) and replaced by MediaPlus provided by Alexander Street (http://alexanderstreet.com/products/mediaplus).

Partners

Historical Texts has been developed by a partnership between Jisc and Knowledge Integration, Gooii, Sero Consulting and the British Library.

The British Library are leading on addressing the challenges of collecting, preserving and providing access to the world's digital knowledge.

Recently digitised books from the British Library 19th century collection are included in Historical Texts.

Knowledge Integration specialises in the development of high quality open source products and components based around open standards and specifications.

Knowledge Integration, along with their partners Sero and Gooii, worked with Jisc to build Historical Texts.
