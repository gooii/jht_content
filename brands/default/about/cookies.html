Cookies

This cookies notice was last updated on 12 November 2019.

To help improve this site as well as enable access to restricted content we place small files, known as cookies, onto your computer.

We use cookies to:

  • enable you to login to access subscription-only content
  • enable you to save searches and items as well as set display preferences
  • remember if you’ve completed a user satisfaction survey, or requested not to be asked to complete it, so you don’t get asked again
  • measure how you use the website so we can provide usage statistics as well as improve it to meet your needs
  • enable us to embed YouTube videos on the website showing you how to use the service

You can manage these small files as you wish. You can even decide to stop any site from using them.

More information can be found at aboutcookies.org or allaboutcookies.org. You can also read the Jisc web team's blog on cookie law.

How we use cookies

We use cookies for several purposes on this website. Some are essential to enable the platform to function, others are not essential but help to improve the user experience, enable us to provide usage statistics, help us to understand how the platform is used and provide the ability to embed videos on our support pages showing you how to use the service.

We’ve listed each of them below with more details about why we use them and how long they’ll last.

Essential cookies

Cookies for authentication

These cookies are required to enable the platform to function, to enable you to log in to access subscription-only content as well as store saved searches, records and tags. If you do not accept these cookies then you will not be able to log in to the platform via the UK Access Management Federation (Shibboleth and OpenAthens).

Session cookies are cleared when you close your browser. Cookies are part of the user identification process and without these you will not be able to authenticate via the UK Access Management Federation and use the service. 1 hour

Non-essential cookies

Cookies for survey

User satisfaction surveys are usually only on the website for around a month once a year.

Name Purpose Expires
COOKIE_NAME_TBC To record whether you have completed the user satisfaction survey. This is to ensure you do not keep getting the survey prompt each time you go to our home page. If you complete the survey the cookie expires at the end of the survey period. If you select 'not today' the cookie expires 24 hours later.
